Suitable Specimen Criteria

In order for the USGS National Wildlife Health Center (NWHC) to provide the best possible diagnostic services, please review the following criteria regarding specimen suitability for diagnostic evaluation.

  • Free-living wildlife under federal, state, or tribal management authority (e.g., birds, mammals, reptiles, amphibians, echinoderms, mollusks, and corals).
    • Wildlife in rehab <72 hours that did not receive antibiotics or other drugs might be suitable. Please provide details on rehabilitation in the comment field before submitting the diagnostic request.
  • Whole carcasses (2-3 per species, usually no more than 5-6 total) with intact body cavity (no or very light scavenging, not previously necropsied).
    • If the carcass is >100lbs or too large to ship please make a note in the comment field before submitting the diagnostic request.
  • Freshly dead or freshly euthanized with none of the following present: slippage of the fur/feathers/scales and skin with gentle rubbing; pronounced odor; green, black or discolored tissues; mushy carcass; extensive maggot or insect activity.
    • If euthanized, please be sure to indicate method of euthanasia in the comment field.
  • Unknown cause of death.
  • Not part of an active state or federal legal investigation. USFWS Special Agents with a specific request, please call NWHC at 608-270-2480; for NWHC Hawaii call 808-792-9520 to discuss.
  • Collector possesses the appropriate state and federal permits for specimen collection and shipment.

If you have suitable carcasses newly in hand and are submitting this request:
Sunday - Wednesday, please hold carcasses chilled.
Thursday - Saturday, please hold carcasses frozen.

If you need field or management guidance regarding selecting carcasses (e.g., species and number to collect), packaging and shipping, disease response consultation, or safety information, please make a note in the comments field and select “Request diagnostic and consultative services”.

If you do not have or can not obtain suitable specimens but would like a disease response consultation, make a note in the comments field and select “Request Consultative Services ONLY”.

If this is an urgent wildlife disease situation, call NWHC at 608-270-2480; for NWHC Hawaii call 808-792-9520.
